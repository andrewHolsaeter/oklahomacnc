Machining fit

The process should match the part, not the other way around.

3-axis when it is the practical choice

Good for plates, fixture components, brackets, pockets, hole patterns, and medium/large parts that do not need complex indexing.

5-axis when setup reduction matters

Useful for smaller complex parts, angled features, multi-sided work, tight access, and geometry that benefits from fewer setups.

Inspection where it counts

Critical dimensions, fits, and functional features are checked so the delivered part is tied back to the real requirement.

Engineering-first approach

Built by someone who has designed, tested, and lived with experimental hardware.

Owner Andrew Holsaeter brings a decade of R&D experience from offshore drilling work off the coast of Norway, including design, prototyping, manufacturing, testing, and publishing technical work around experimental parts and sensors.

That background shapes the shop’s approach: ask the right questions early, call out manufacturability issues before they become expensive, and keep the process practical for prototypes and small-batch work.
